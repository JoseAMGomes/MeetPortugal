Lisbon

Why should i visit Lisbon?

Lisbon is the capital and the largest city of Portugal surrounded by cinematic hillsides overlooking the Tejo river, ancient ruins and white-domed cathedrals. Lisbon is a city where you feel safe wandering around day or night, where the cuisine is dedicated to creating a thousand ways to cook salted cod (bacalhau) and where you will find hotels and restaurant for all budgets.

Which are Lisbon's main atractions?

Torre de Belém (A)

Belém Tower or officially the Tower of Saint Vincent is a 16th-century fortification that served as a point of embarkation and disembarkation for Portuguese explorers and as a ceremonial gateway to Lisbon. This delightful little fort guards the Tejo Estuary and is decorated by Arabic styled watchtowers and battlements adorned with the “Cross of Christ”.

Mosteiro dos Jerónimos (B)

This is an Unesco-listed monastery. The religious building was designed by the Portuguese architect Diogo de Boitaca to commemorate the return of Vasco da Gama from India. The Jerónimos Monastery replaced the church formerly existing in the same place, which was dedicated to Santa Maria de Belém and where the monks of the military-religious Order of Christ provided assistance to seafarers in transit.

Parque das Nações (C)

Known as Expo (as the site of the 1998 Lisbon World Exposition), it is a wonderful place to walk around at any time of the year.
