Porto

Why should i visit Porto?

Porto is the second largest city in Portugal. This is the city that gave its name to Porto wine. It’s an age-old city that was built on the hills overlooking the Douro River. Porto is a fascinating city surrounded not only by old narrow streets in Ribeira, but also by grand plazas of Trindade and beaches and ocean views of the Foz district.

Which are Porto's main atractions?

A few tourist attractions you may find interesting are:
Torre dos Clérigos(A) - The Clérigos Church is a Baroque church. Its tall bell tower, the Torre dos Clérigos, can be seen from various points of the city and is one of its most characteristic symbols.
Avenida dos Aliados(B) - This is considered Porto’s city centre. This avenue is impressive with their different architectural buildings. Its name translates to “Avenue of the Allies” that refers to the treaty between Portugal and United Kingdom in the 14th century. Casa da Música(C) - This is a concert hall with a very interesting arquitecture designed by Rem Koolhaas.
