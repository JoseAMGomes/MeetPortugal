Madeira

Why should i visit Madeira?

Madeira is one of the Portugal islands in Atlantic known by its wine, beautiful landscapes and botanic gardens. This is one of the most iconic places in the world to spend New Year’s Eve with their spectacular fireworks.

Which are Madeira's main atractions?

A few tourist attractions you may find interesting are:
Pico ruivo (A)- Pico Ruivo is the highest peak on Madeira island. It can be reached only by foot, usually either from Pico do Arieiro after a strenuous hike, or from Achada do Teixeira via a shorter, easier trail. The distance in one direction is about 7 km but it takes about 3 hours. Pico ruivo is 1,861 metres (6,106 ft) high and provides panoramic views of the island from coast to coast.
São Vicente (B)- São Vicente is an area characterized by green nature and abundant forest, modelled by volcanic forces and erosion in its beautiful slopes. One of the most representative symbols of the picturesque town of São Vicente is the small chapel built inside a basalt rock at the mouth of the stream that runs through the town.
Câmara de lobos (C)- Câmara de Lobos is a pretty fishing town that is located on the south-eastern side of Madeira and makes for a pleasant day trip from Funchal. It is a picturesque location with colourful fishing boats pulled up onto the grey beaches, which are over looked by some of the highest cliffs in Europe. You can explore the fisherman’s chapel where the wives of fishermen would come and pray for their husbands while they were out on the dangerous waters. You can walk on the glass bottomed skywalk at the Cabo Girao cliffs with a 500m drop.
